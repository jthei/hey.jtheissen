#### An attempt to collect things I know and that you may be interested in Hey Theissen, [Where can I find ...?](#where) Hey Theissen, [What is the standard size for ...?](#standards) Hey Theissen, [Help me choose a ...](#choose) Hey Theissen, [How do I ...?](#how) Hey Theissen, [Inspire Me!](#inspire) --- ####<a id="where"></a>Where can I find ...? **Web Dev/Design Frameworks &amp; Tools** - [Bootstrap](http://getbootstrap.com/) - The most popular HTML, CSS, and JS framework for developing responsive, mobile first projects on the web. Cuts a couple weeks off of your design cycle and is pretty standardized as long as you stick to best practices. People should be able to understand and use your site at first glance rather than being lost. - [jQuery](https://jquery.com/) - jQuery is a fast, small, and feature-rich JavaScript library. Extensive Documentation on the site. - [SPServices](http://spservices.codeplex.com/) - Client-side SharePoint interaction with jQuery. - [repl.it](http://repl.it/languages/JavaScript) - An online environment for interactively exploring programming languages. It provides a fully-featured terminal emulator and code editor, powered by interpreter engines for more than 15 languages. I use it to test out Javascript functions on the fly, but it does so much more. - [StackOverflow](http://stackoverflow.com/) - The answer to every question you could ever hope to ask, especially in regards to web development. - [Github](https://github.com/) - GitHub is how people build software. A combination of social media network and version control system. Tons of code that is free to use (mostly) and learn from as well as hosting and maintaining your own projects. - [regex101](https://regex101.com/#javascript) - Regular Expression Tester, you should learn regular expressions regardless of what you do. It'll make you a better person. - [extendsclass](https://extendsclass.com/) - A collection of free developer tools: testers (RegEx, XPath, JSONPath, SQLite), data generators (CSV, JSON, SQL), diff (XML, JSON, CSV), code checkers (Python, PHP, Ruby). **Photographs (Free Stock):** - [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/) - Free High Resolution Photos - [Stock Xchng](http://www.sxc.hu/) - A free-to-use collection of royalty free images - [Wikimedia Commons](http://commons.wikimedia.org) - Wiki style collection of images, icons, and various other media available under a creative commons license (often simple attribution). A terrific resource for finding things quickly and inexpensively. - [TinEye](http://tineye.com) - If you have a picture and you need a higher resolution or need to know where it came from, search here. **Fonts:** - [Font Squirrel](www.fontsquirrel.com/) - Excellent, curated, collection of free fonts for graphic designers. - [Google Fonts](https://www.google.com/fonts) - Free fonts for web-based projects **Icons:** - [Noun Project](http://thenounproject.com) - An attempt to create basic, language agnostic, icons for nearly every*thing* in the world. Even if you don't like the icons they provide, it is a good place to look for simple ways to express objects and concepts. - [IconFinder](http://iconfinder.com) - Basic search application for sifting through thousands of icons. Offers the ability to sort by price and license. - [Wikimedia Commons](http://commons.wikimedia.org) - Wiki style collection of images, icons, and various other media available under a creative commons license (often simple attribution). A terrific resource for finding things quickly and inexpensively. - [We Love Icon Fonts](http://weloveiconfonts.com/) - Free &amp; open source icon fonts hosting service (like Google Web Fonts, but icon fonts only). - [FontAwesome](http://fortawesome.github.io/Font-Awesome/icons/) - Fantastic Icon Font, better than Bootstraps built-in Glyphicons. **Official Brand Logos:** - [Brands of the World](http://brandsoftheworld.com) - A massive collection of many of the world's top brand marks in vector format often with correct spot colors. Important for maintaining accurate brand representation. **Some General Design Assets** - [ByPeople](http://www.bypeople.com/) - a massive (5GB) bundle of *mostly* web design assets, many of which can be used for other screen based projects as well. --- ####<a id="standards"></a>What is the standard size for ...? - [Frame Sizes](http://www.logangraphic.com/how-to/framesizes.php) - We typically use 11 x 14", **16 x 20"**, or 24 x 36" - [Envelopes](http://designerstoolbox.com/designresources/envelopes/) - For invitations, we typically use a *5 Baronial* which is 5.5 x 4.125" and will accomodate a 5.25 x 3.875" invite. - [Paper](http://designerstoolbox.com/designresources/paper/) - Letter: 8.5 x 11", Tabloid 11 x 17", Legal: 8.5 x 14" - [More](http://designerstoolbox.com/) --- ####<a id="choose"></a>Help me choose a ... **Color** - [Colour Lovers](http://www.colourlovers.com/) - User created pallettes with excellent search and discovery tools. - [ColorSchemer](http://colorschemedesigner.com/) - I'm partial to a certain orange that lives at #ff9900. - [Adobe Color](https://color.adobe.com/) - If you need someone else to give you some colors (formerly called Adobe Kuler). **Typeface** - [WhattheFont](http://www.myfonts.com/WhatTheFont/) - Trying to find out what font someone used? Scan a sample and search here. - [I Love Typography](http://ilovetypography.com/) - Review and criticism of new typefaces and trends in typography. Lots of info. **Layout** - [Balsamiq](http://balsamiq.com/) - Practice your mock-ups/wireframes online (built for web/software interface but do with it what you will) - [Tschichold or Van de Graaf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canons_of_page_construction) - There are a million different opinions on how to layout a page and it differs based on your end goal, be it a book, a pamphlet, a poster, etc. But I, personally, tend to lean towards my good buddy Jan Tschichold's ideas and the golden mean. - [Golden Ratio Calculator](http://goldenratiocalculator.com/) - When in doubt, Golden Ratio always looks nice. --- ####<a id="how"></a>How do I ...? **Deal with Data** - [Alphabetizer!](http://alphabetizer.flap.tv/) - Alphabetize and format a list of data in a variety of ways - [Spreeder](http://spreeder.com/) - Speed reading assistant. --- ####<a id="inspire"></a>Inspire Me! **Design In General** - [How Design Works](http://startupsthisishowdesignworks.com/) - A guide for people outside of the field of design, specifically start-up businesses, but very pertinent to how we - as designers - should be focusing our practice. It's important to look beyond the pixels and see the actual problem your design is trying to solve. - [Skills Designers Must Master](http://www.aiga.org/beyond-craft-and-tools-the-skills-design-students-must-master/) - AIGA's look at the critical skills design students (if you are a designer, you never stop being a student) need to master. - [How Designers Destroyed the World](https://vimeo.com/122022963) - Designer Mike Monteiro delivers an empassioned talk about our responsibility as designers. **Logo/Icon Design** - [Logo Design Love](http://www.logodesignlove.com/) - Exhibition and criticism of the design of logos and brand identities. Edited by [David Airey](http://davidairey.com). - [Noun Project](http://thenounproject.com) - An attempt to create basic, language agnostic, icons for nearly every*thing* in the world. Even if you don't like the icons they provide, it is a good place to look for simple ways to express objects and concepts. **[More](http://www.inspirationlinks.com/)** --- ###Anything worth doing, is worth *over* doing.
